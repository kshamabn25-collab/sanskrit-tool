⚠️ macOS says "damaged or can't be opened"? Click here

This is a macOS security warning for apps not yet verified by Apple. The app is completely safe. Here's how to open it:

Method 1 — Easiest (System Settings)

  1. When the "damaged" dialog appears, click Cancel
  2. Click the Apple menu (🍎) in the top-left corner of your screen
  3. Click System Settings
  4. Click Privacy & Security in the left sidebar
  5. Scroll down until you see a message like "Script Tool was blocked"
  6. Click Open Anyway — then click Open in the next popup
  7. That's it! The app will open and you won't see this warning again ✅

Method 2 — Right-click trick

  1. Open your Applications folder (Finder → Applications)
  2. Right-click (or hold Control and click) on Script Tool
  3. Choose Open from the menu
  4. A new dialog appears — click Open again
  5. Done! ✅

Method 3 — Terminal (one command)

  1. Press Cmd + Space, type Terminal, press Enter
  2. Copy and paste this command, then press Enter:
    xattr -cr "/Applications/Script Tool.app"
  3. Close Terminal and double-click the app — it opens normally ✅

What it does

⌨️

Sanskrit IME — type anywhere

Toggle a system-wide Sanskrit input mode with ⌥⇧M. Type phonetic Roman in any app — WhatsApp, Notion, Word, browser — and get Indic script inserted directly, no copy-paste needed.

🔡

7 output scripts

Devanagari, Kannada, Telugu, Tamil, Bengali, Malayalam, and IAST. Switch scripts instantly from the tray menu or with ⌥⇧O.

🔤

3 input schemes

ITRANS, Harvard-Kyoto, and SLP1 — the most widely used phonetic Sanskrit schemes. Switch on the fly with ⌥⇧1 / 2 / 3.

🪟

Quick panel

Press ⌥⇧S for a floating mini-panel — type a word, preview the output, press Enter to paste. Disappears when done.

✈️

100% offline

No internet required. No data leaves your machine. Works on planes, in remote areas, everywhere.

📌

Lives in the menu bar

Always one click away. No dock clutter. Launches at login so it's always ready.

How it works

1

Install & launch

Download and run the installer. Script Tool appears in your menu bar automatically and launches at login.

2

Grant Accessibility permission (macOS IME)

On first launch, macOS will prompt you to grant Accessibility access in System Settings → Privacy & Security → Accessibility. This is required for the IME to intercept keystrokes system-wide.

3

Toggle the Sanskrit IME

Press ⌥⇧M — a small status bar appears at the top of your screen. Click into any text field in any app and start typing phonetic Sanskrit.

4

Type and commit words

Type namaskaara → the status bar shows namaskaara → नमस्कार live. Press Space to insert the converted word and continue, or Esc to cancel.

5

Use the converter window or quick panel

Press ⌥⇧I for the full converter window, or ⌥⇧S for a floating quick panel — type, preview, press Enter to paste into whatever app is behind it.
