⚠️ macOS says "damaged or can't be opened"? Click here

This is a macOS security warning for apps not yet verified by Apple. The app is completely safe. Here's how to open it:

Method 1 — Easiest (System Settings)

  1. When the "damaged" dialog appears, click Cancel
  2. Click the Apple menu (🍎) in the top-left corner of your screen
  3. Click System Settings
  4. Click Privacy & Security in the left sidebar
  5. Scroll down until you see a message like "Script Tool was blocked"
  6. Click Open Anyway — then click Open in the next popup
  7. That's it! The app will open and you won't see this warning again ✅

Method 2 — Right-click trick

  1. Open your Applications folder (Finder → Applications)
  2. Right-click (or hold Control and click) on Script Tool
  3. Choose Open from the menu
  4. A new dialog appears — click Open again
  5. Done! ✅

Method 3 — Terminal (one command)

  1. Press Cmd + Space, type Terminal, press Enter
  2. Copy and paste this command, then press Enter:
    xattr -cr "/Applications/Script Tool.app"
  3. Close Terminal and double-click the app — it opens normally ✅

What it does

⌨️

Phonetic → 6 Scripts

Type ITRANS, Harvard-Kyoto, or SLP1 and get live output in Devanagari, Kannada, Telugu, Tamil, Bengali, or Malayalam.

🔤

Indic → IAST

Paste any Indic script text and instantly get the IAST (International Alphabet of Sanskrit Transliteration) equivalent.

📋

One-click Copy

Copy the output to your clipboard with a single click. Paste anywhere — documents, emails, code.

📌

Lives in the Menu Bar

Always one click away in your system tray. No dock clutter. Launches on hotkey.

✈️

100% Offline

No internet connection required. No data leaves your machine. Works anywhere.

🆓

Free & Open Source

MIT licensed. No subscriptions, no accounts, no telemetry.

How it works

1

Install & launch

Download the installer for your platform and run it. Script Tool appears in your menu bar / system tray automatically.

2

Open with a hotkey or click

Press Alt+Shift+I to open the tool — or just click the tray icon.

3

Type, convert, copy

Phonetic mode: type namaskaara → pick a script → output appears live. Click Copy.
IAST mode: paste Indic text → IAST appears instantly. Click Copy.

4

Paste anywhere

The result is on your clipboard. Paste it into any app — Word, Google Docs, browser, code editor.
